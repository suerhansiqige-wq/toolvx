Fake Redaction vs. True Redaction

Many "free PDF editors" let you draw black rectangles over text—but that is annotation, not redaction. The original characters remain in the PDF object stream; anyone can select, copy, or extract them with free utilities. True redaction destroys the underlying content at the pixel or glyph level so recovery is impractical. Before trusting any tool, export a test page, try to select text beneath your mask, and run a quick text-extraction check in your PDF viewer's search box.

Browser-based local tools like toolvx rasterize redacted regions when you apply mosaic, blur, or blackout effects, which removes selectable text in those areas. This approach suits bank statements, medical intake forms, and legal exhibits where you need verifiable sanitization without installing Adobe Acrobat Pro. For multi-page files, work page-by-page and confirm headers and footers on every sheet—account numbers often repeat on each page of a statement.

A Reliable PDF Redaction Checklist

Before sending any redacted PDF, walk through this five-point check: (1) Search the document for known sensitive strings—your SSN fragment, account suffix, or company EIN. (2) Zoom to 200% on every redaction box; extend selections if anti-aliased edges peek through. (3) Review all pages including appendices and blank-looking pages that may contain embedded images of checks or IDs. (4) Use blackout for financial digits; mosaic or blur is acceptable only for non-critical visual elements like background faces. (5) Save under a new filename (e.g., statement-redacted.pdf) so you never accidentally attach the original.
