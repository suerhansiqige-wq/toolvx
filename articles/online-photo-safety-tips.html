Top 10 Online Photo Safety Tips for 2026

Stay safe while sharing memories online

Introduction

Every day, billions of photos are shared online. While sharing memories is wonderful, each photo carries potential risks. Follow these essential tips to protect yourself and your loved ones.

Tip #1: Always Check Backgrounds

Before posting, scan the background for sensitive information: street addresses, school names, workplace logos, or house numbers.

Tip #2: Get Consent Before Posting Others

Always ask permission before sharing photos containing other people, especially children. Respect their right to privacy.

Tip #3: Remove Location Data

Disable geotagging on your camera and strip location metadata before sharing. Use our free tool to clean images.

Tip #4: Blur Faces When Necessary

Not everyone wants to be online. Blur faces of people who haven't explicitly consented to being photographed.

Tip #5: Avoid Posting in Real-Time

Wait until you've left a location before posting photos. Real-time posts reveal your current whereabouts to potential stalkers.

Tip #6: Use Private Accounts

Keep personal accounts private and carefully vet follower requests. Public accounts expose your photos to anyone.

Tip #7: Watermark Your Work

If you're a photographer, add subtle watermarks to prevent unauthorized use of your professional work.

Tip #8: Be Careful with QR Codes

Never post photos containing QR codes—they could link to malicious websites or contain payment information.

Tip #9: Watch for Reflections

Check mirrors, windows, and sunglasses in photos for reflected screens showing passwords or sensitive info.

Tip #10: Regularly Audit Your Photos

Periodically review old posts and delete photos that may pose privacy risks as circumstances change.
