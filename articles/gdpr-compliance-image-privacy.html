GDPR Compliance for Images: Complete Legal Guide

Understanding your legal obligations when publishing photos in the EU

Are Faces Considered Personal Data Under GDPR?

Yes! Under GDPR Article 4, facial images are considered biometric data and qualify as personal data. This means you must have a lawful basis (usually explicit consent) before publishing someone's photo.

Key Point:

Even if you don't name someone, their face alone can identify them, making it personal data under GDPR.

When Do You Need Consent?

Scenario Consent Required?
Company website team photos YES - Written consent
Event photography (public) Maybe - Depends on context
News/journalism Exemption may apply
Blurred/anonymized images NO - Not personal data

How to Comply: Practical Steps

  1. 1. Obtain Written Consent

    Use clear language explaining where and how the photo will be used

  2. 2. Provide Opt-Out Options

    Allow people to withdraw consent at any time
